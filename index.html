The Plan

10 Days in Japan

A richer, city-focused trip — five days to really live in Tokyo, two for the soul of Kyoto, three to enjoy Osaka without rushing. Each day is split into morning, lunch, afternoon and dinner, with travel times and the daily intensity marked clearly.

Before you go

Essential Tips

🎟️
Pre-book immediately
USJ + Nintendo World, Shibuya Sky, Rokkasen (if you go), TeamLab. All sell out weeks ahead in August. Book the moment you confirm.
💴
Money & IC cards
Get Suica/Pasmo at Narita airport — tap on/off every train and convenience store. Japan is still cash-heavy; bring ¥50,000/person/week. ATMs at 7-Eleven work with foreign cards.
🚄
Shinkansen
Register at smart-ex.jp before you leave Greece. Book Hikari (¥14,170/person Tokyo→Kyoto). Seats D and E for Mt Fuji views on the right side. Departs from Tokyo Station or Shinagawa.
🚲
Kyoto e-bikes
Book through your Kyoto hotel on arrival. Distances and hills make e-bikes essential. Our friend is emphatic — it completely changes the Kyoto experience.
📖
Eki stamps
Buy a small notebook at any station shop on day 1. Stamp at every JR station you pass through — free, unique per city, a perfect trip-long souvenir for the kids.
📱
Essential apps
USJ Official app (Nintendo World entry), Tokyo Disney app, Google Maps (works perfectly in Japan), HyperDia or Navitime for train routes. Download before leaving Greece.
🧳
Luggage forwarding
Yamato Transport "Takkyubin" — send bags from Tokyo hotel to Kyoto hotel overnight (¥1,500–2,000/bag, book the night before). Travel completely light on the Shinkansen.
🌡️
August heat
32–36°C and humid. Small towel, hand fan, sunscreen every day. Visit temples before 10am or after 4pm. Cold drinks from convenience stores are your best friends all day.
📋
Japan entry QR
Register at Visit Japan Web before departure — saves time at the airport and is used for VAT refunds in shops. Bring passports when shopping for refunds.
🍬
Okashi no Machioka
Japan's discount snack chain — walls of unique treats at ¥50–300. Perfect for cheap omiyage (gifts) for friends and colleagues back home. Branches everywhere in Tokyo and Osaka.
🚫
Street rules
No bins on the street — use 7-Eleven/Lawson bins. No smoking on streets. Eating while walking is frowned upon. Queue on the left even in the subway. All small gestures that locals notice.
🍣
Restaurant bookings
Book advance restaurants via Jpneazy or Tablecheck. Wagyu Yakiniku Waya (Kyoto), any Michelin spots — reserve weeks ahead. Most casual ramen and market spots are walk-in.
